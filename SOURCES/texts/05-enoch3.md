## PART II: TRANSLATION

### Chapter I

**R. Ishmael ascends to heaven to behold the vision of the Merkaba and is given in charge to Metatron**

*"And Enoch walked with God: and he was not; for God took him" (Gen. v. 24)*

Rabbi Ishmael said:

(1) When I ascended on high to behold the vision of the Merkaba and had entered the six Halls, one within the other:

(2) as soon as I reached the door of the seventh Hall I stood still in prayer before the Holy One, blessed be He, and, lifting up my eyes on high (i.e. towards the Divine Majesty), I said:

(3) "Lord of the Universe, I pray thee, that the merit of Aaron, the son of Amram, the lover of peace and pursuer of peace, who received the crown of priesthood from Thy Glory on the mount of Sinai, be valid for me in this hour, so that Qafsiel, the prince, and the angels with him may not get power over me nor throw me down from the heavens."

(4) Forthwith the Holy One, blessed be He, sent to me Metatron, his Servant, the angel, the Prince of the Presence, and he, spreading his wings, with great joy came to meet me so as to save me from their hand.

(5) And he took me by his hand in their sight, saying to me:

"Enter in peace before the high and exalted King and behold the picture of the Merkaba."

(6) Then I entered the seventh Hall, and he led me to the camps of Shekina and placed me before the Holy One, blessed be He, to behold the Merkaba.

(7) As soon as the princes of the Merkaba and the flaming Seraphim perceived me, they fixed their eyes upon me. Instantly trembling and shuddering seized me and I fell down and was benumbed by the radiant image of their eyes and the splendid appearance of their faces; until the Holy One, blessed be He, rebuked them, saying:

(8) "My servants, my Seraphim, my Kerubim and my 'Ophannim! Cover ye your eyes before Ishmael, my son, my friend, my beloved one and my glory, that he tremble not nor shudder!"

(9) Forthwith Metatron the Prince of the Presence, came and restored my spirit and put me upon my feet.

(10) After that moment there was not in me strength enough to say a song before the Throne of Glory of the glorious King, the mightiest of all kings, the most excellent of all princes, until after the hour had passed.

(11) After one hour had passed the Holy One, blessed be He, opened to me the gates of Shekina, the gates of Peace, the gates of Wisdom, the gates of Strength, the gates of Power, the gates of Speech, the gates of Song, the gates of Qedushsha, the gates of Chant.

(12) And he enlightened my eyes and my heart by words of psalm, song, praise, exaltation, thanksgiving, extolment, glorification, hymn and eulogy. And as I opened my mouth, uttering a song before the Holy One, blessed be He, the Holy Chayyoth beneath and above the Throne of Glory answered and said: "HOLY" and "BLESSED BE THE GLORY OF YHWH FROM HIS PLACE!" (i.e. chanted the Qedushsha).

### Chapter II

**The highest classes of angels make inquiries about R. Ishmael, which are answered by Metatron**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) In that hour the eagles of the Merkaba, the flaming 'Ophannim and the Seraphim of consuming fire asked Metatron, saying to him:

(2) "Youth! Why sufferest thou one born of woman to enter and behold the Merkaba? From which nation, from which tribe is this one? What is his character?"

Metatron answered and said to them:

(3) "He is from the nation of Israel whom the Holy One, blessed be He, chose for his people from among seventy tongues (nations), from the tribe of Levi, whom he set aside as a contribution to his name and from the seed of Aaron whom the Holy One, blessed be He, did choose for his servant and put upon him the crown of priesthood."

(4) Forthwith they spake and said:

"Indeed, this one is worthy to behold the Merkaba."

As it is written: "Happy is the people that is in such a case!" (Ps. cxliv. 15).

---

### Chapter III

**Metatron has 70 names, but God calls him 'Youth'**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) In that hour I asked Metatron, the angel, the Prince of the Presence: "What is thy name?"

(2) He answered me: "I have seventy names, corresponding to the seventy tongues of the world and all of them are based upon the name Metatron, angel of the Presence; but my King calls me 'Youth' (Na'ar)."

---

### Chapter IV

**Metatron is identical with Enoch who was translated to heaven at the time of the Deluge**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) I asked Metatron and said to him: "Why art thou called by the name of thy Creator, by seventy names? Thou art greater than all the princes, higher than all the angels, beloved more than all the servants, honoured above all the mighty ones in kingship, greatness and glory: why do they call thee 'Youth' in the high heavens?"

(2) He answered and said to me: "Because I am Enoch, the son of Jared.

(3) For when the generation of the flood sinned and were confounded in their deeds, saying unto God: 'Depart from us, for we desire not the knowledge of thy ways' (Job xxi. 14), then the Holy One, blessed be He, removed me from their midst to be a witness against them in the high heavens to all the inhabitants of the world, that they may not say: 'The Merciful One is cruel.'

What sinned all those multitudes, their wives, their sons and their daughters, their horses, their mules and their cattle and their property, and all the birds of the world, all of which the Holy One, blessed be He, destroyed from the world together with them in the waters of the flood?

Or, let it be they sinned, what had their sons and their daughters, their mules and their cattle sinned? And likewise, all the animals, domestic and wild, and the birds in the world."

(4) Then the Holy One, blessed be He, answered and said: "I will make him (Enoch) a prince and a ruler over all the inhabitants of the world, and he shall be a witness against them to all the inhabitants of the world.

(5) And I will give him a throne (kisse) like My throne, and he shall sit upon it in the high heavens.

(6) And I will give him a crown like My crown, and he shall wear it upon his head.

(7) And I will give him a name like My name, and he shall be called by it in all the world."

(8) Then the Holy One, blessed be He, took him up and set him in the high heavens, and gave him a throne at the right hand of the Throne of Glory.

(9) And He called him by seventy names, and all of them were like the names of the Holy One, blessed be He.

(10) And He called him "Youth" (Na'ar) because he was young in the days of his translation, and because he was the youngest among all the angels and princes that were created in the six days of creation.

(11) And He made him greater than all the angels, and his name is greater than all the names that are in the entire world, except the Name of the Holy One, blessed be He.

---

### Chapter V

**The Prince of the World and the Seventy Nations**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) Metatron said to me: "I have seventy names, corresponding to the seventy nations of the world.

(2) And for each nation I have a name, and by that name I call it in the high heavens.

(3) And I am set over all the nations of the world, and I distribute to them their sustenance."

(4) And I said to him: "What is the name of the first nation?"

(5) He answered and said: "The first nation is the Roman Empire, and its name is 'Rome.'

(6) And the second nation is the Persian Empire, and its name is 'Persia.'

(7) And the third nation is the Greek Empire, and its name is 'Greece.'

(8) And so on, until the seventy nations."

(9) And I said to him: "Who is the prince over the Roman Empire?"

(10) He answered and said: "Sariel is his name, and he is set over the Roman Empire."

(11) And I said to him: "Who is the prince over the Persian Empire?"

(12) He answered and said: "Michael is his name, and he is set over the Persian Empire."

(13) And so on, for all the seventy nations.

---

### Chapter VI

**The Celestial Chariot (Merkaba)**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) I saw the Merkaba, and it was like a great throne, prepared for the Holy One, blessed be He.

(2) And upon the throne sat One whose appearance was like a man, and His glory was immeasurable.

(3) And around about Him stood the seraphim, the ofannim, the cherubim, and the living creatures.

(4) And the seraphim had six wings, and with two they covered their faces, and with two they covered their feet, and with two they flew.

(5) And they called one to another and said: "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord of hosts: the whole earth is full of His glory."

(6) And the ofannim had four wings, and they also sang the trisagion.

(7) And the cherubim had four faces, four wings, and the hands of a man under their wings.

(8) And the living creatures had the faces of a lion, an ox, a man, and an eagle.

(9) And they sang before the Throne, and their voice was like the voice of many waters, and like the voice of thunder.

(10) And the Throne was covered with a curtain of clouds and a covering of glory.

(11) And the angels of the Presence stood round about it, and they sang hymns and songs to the Holy One, blessed be He.

---

### Chapter VII

**The Twelve Months and their Princes**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) Metatron said to me: "There are twelve months, and for each month there is a prince, and the prince is set over the month.

(2) And the first month is Nisan, and its prince is Michael.

(3) And the second month is Iyar, and its prince is Gabriel.

(4) And the third month is Sivan, and its prince is Nissan.

(5) And the fourth month is Tammuz, and its prince is Yefefia.

(6) And the fifth month is Av, and its prince is Zuriel.

(7) And the sixth month is Elul, and its prince is Mikhiel.

(8) And the seventh month is Tishrei, and its prince is Rafael.

(9) And the eighth month is Cheshvan, and its prince is Barkhiel.

(10) And the ninth month is Kislev, and its prince is Za'piel.

(11) And the tenth month is Tevet, and its prince is Shakiel.

(12) And the eleventh month is Shevat, and its prince is Tagdiel.

(13) And the twelfth month is Adar, and its prince is Azriel."

---

### Chapter VIII

**The Angels and their Functions**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) Metatron said to me: "There are ten princes (serarim) over the angels, and they are:

1. Gabriel - who is set over the strength of the world
2. Michael - who is set over the wisdom of the world
3. Sariel - who is set over the nobility of the world
4. Raphael - who is set over the healing of the world
5. Zadkiel - who is set over the goodness of the world
6. Hadarniel - who is set over the mercy of the world
7. Kafziel - who is set over the prayers of the children of men
8. Peniel - who is set over the repentance of the sinners
9. Akatriel - who is set over the crown of the Shekina
10. Metatron - who is set over the Torah of the children of men"

(2) And each one has under him thousands of thousands and myriads of myriads of ministering angels.

(3) And they all stand before the Throne of Glory, and they sing hymns and songs to the Holy One, blessed be He.

---

### Chapter IX

**The Celestial Song**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) Metatron said to me: "The holy angels sing the song of the seraphim, and the ofannim answer them, and the cherubim respond to them, and the living creatures repeat it after them.

(2) And the sound of their voices goes forth into all the worlds, and it is heard by every living creature that has breath in its nostrils.

(3) And the song that they sing is this: 'Holy, holy, holy is the Lord of hosts: the whole earth is full of His glory.'

(4) And they sing it three times a day:

- At the hour of midnight, when the Holy One, blessed be He, sits upon the Throne of Judgment
- At the hour of sunrise, when the Holy One, blessed be He, sits upon the Throne of Mercy
- At the hour of sunset, when the Holy One, blessed be He, sits upon the Throne of Glory"

(5) And when the holy angels sing this song, the whole world trembles, and the foundations of the earth shake, and the stars of heaven quake, and the sun and moon cease from their courses.

(6) And the holy angels are not able to finish their song, for the sound of their voices shakes the world and all that is in it.

---

### Chapter X

**The Secrets of Metatron**

R. Ishmael said:

(1) Metatron said to me: "I am the messenger of the Holy One, blessed be He, and I am set over all the mysteries of the Torah.

(2) And I reveal to you the secrets of the Torah, and the hidden things of the world to come.

(3) And I tell you of the angels that are set over the world, and of the princes that are appointed over the nations.

(4) And I show you the path of the just, and the way of the righteous, and the road of them that return to the Holy One, blessed be He."

(5) And the children of men heard these things, and they were amazed, and they said: "Who is like unto Metatron, the angel of the Great Counsel, who reveals the secrets of the Most High?"

(6) And Metatron answered and said: "There is none like unto me, for I am the copy of the Divine Presence, and I am the messenger of the Holy One, blessed be He."

(7) And the children of men asked him: "How long shall we have to live in this world?"

(8) And Metatron answered and said: "Repent, and do good works, and the Holy One, blessed be He, will lengthen your days, and you shall inherit the World to Come."

## APPENDICES

### On the Origin of the Word "Metatron"

Various theories have been proposed regarding the etymology of "Metatron":

1. From the Greek "meta" (with) and "thronos" (throne) - "one who sits beside the throne"
2. From the Hebrew "shomer" (guard) - "one who guards"
3. From the Aramaic "mattara" - "measure" or "bounds"

The most widely accepted view connects it to the Greek concept of one who presides over measures or counts.

### On the Conceptions of Metatron

Metatron occupies a unique position in Jewish angelology:

- He is identified with the biblical Enoch
- He serves as the celestial scribe
- He is called "the lesser YHWH" (YHWH ha-qatan)
- He mediates between God and humanity
- He presides over the Torah in heaven
- He is the Prince of the World
- He has seventy names corresponding to the seventy nations

### On the Angelology of 3 Enoch

The angelology of 3 Enoch includes several distinct classes of angels:

**The Four Living Creatures (Chayyoth)**
- Have four faces: lion, ox, man, eagle
- Support the Throne of Glory
- Sing the Qedushsha

**The Seraphim**
- Have six wings
- Sing "Holy, holy, holy"
- Guard the Throne

**The Cherubim**
- Have four faces and four wings
- Have hands of a man under their wings
- Guard the secrets of God

**The Ofannim**
- Have four wings
- Are the "wheels" of the chariot
- Sing hymns to God

### On the Heavenly Sanctuaries (Hekhalot)

The book describes seven halls (heikhalot) arranged in concentric circles:

1. The first hall - entrance for the righteous
2. The second hall - for the intermediate righteous
3. The third hall - for those who repented
4. The fourth hall - for the penitent
5. The fifth hall - for the slightly righteous
6. The sixth hall - for the more righteous
7. The seventh hall - where the Merkaba and Throne of Glory are located
