# Book of Changes (Yijing)

## What this source gives us

This source is important for the base because it treats reality as a structured field of changes. The 64 hexagrams and 8 trigrams are not just divination tools; they are a compact model of order, transformation, polarity, and repetition.

## Why it matters

- It is one of the strongest ancient sources for number, pattern, and cosmological classification.
- It helps compare Chinese divination with the calendrical and astronomical material in 1 Enoch.
- It gives a non-Western model where the world is read as structured transitions rather than as a single mythic narrative.
- It is useful for the broader theme of hidden knowledge, state-patterns, and the relationship between heaven, order, and rule.

## Key themes to watch

- yin and yang
- 64 states of change
- divination as classification
- cosmic order through pattern
- political order and legitimacy
