# The Paradise of the First Created of Mankind in Eridu

## What this source gives us

This source is not a primary tablet edition, but it is extremely useful as a scholarly gateway into Eridu, Ea/Enki, Adapa, and the Mesopotamian logic of paradise, wisdom, and limited immortality.

## Why it matters

- It ties Eridu to creation, sacred knowledge, and the limits of human life.
- It gives a clear bridge from Mesopotamian wisdom tradition to later comparative discussions about lost immortality.
- It is helpful for the base because Eridu is one of the major sacred-city motifs in Mesopotamian religion.

## Key motifs

- Eridu as sacred center
- Ea/Enki as wise creator
- Adapa as created being with wisdom but no immortality
- knowledge versus everlasting life
- paradise as a cosmic/sacred place
