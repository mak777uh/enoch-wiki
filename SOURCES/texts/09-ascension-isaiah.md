## Part I: The Martyrdom of Isaiah (Chapters I-II)

### Chapter I

1. And it came to pass in the twenty-sixth year of the reign of Hezekiah king of Judah that he called Manasseh his son. Now he was his only one. Isaiah.

2. And he called him into the presence of Isaiah the son of Amoz the prophet, and into the presence of Josib the son of Isaiah, in order to deliver unto him the words of righteousness which the king himself had seen:

3. And of the eternal judgements and the torments of Gehenna, and of the prince of this world, and of his angels, and his authorities and his powers,

4. And the words of the faith of the Beloved which he himself had seen in the fifteenth year of his reign during his illness.

5. And he delivered unto him the written words which Samnas the scribe had written, and also those which Isaiah, the son of Amoz, had given to him, and also to the prophets, that they might write and store up with him what he himself had seen in the king's house regarding the judgement of the angels, and the destruction of this world, and regarding the garments of the saints and their going forth, and regarding their transformation and the persecution and ascension of the Beloved.

6. In the twentieth year of the reign of Hezekiah, Isaiah had seen the words of this prophecy and had delivered them to Josib his son.

7. Isaiah said to Hezekiah the king: 'As the Lord liveth, whose name has not been sent into this world, and as the Beloved of my Lord liveth, and the Spirit which speaketh in me liveth, all these commands and these words will be made of none effect by Manasseh thy son, and through the agency of his hands I shall depart mid the torture of my body.

8. And Sammael Malchira will serve Manasseh, and execute all his desire, and he will become a follower of Beliar rather than of me:

9. And many in Jerusalem and in Judea he will cause to abandon the true faith, and Beliar will dwell in Manasseh, and by his hands I shall be sawn asunder.

10. And when Hezekiah heard these words he wept very bitterly, and rent his garments, and placed earth upon his head, and fell on his face.

11. And Isaiah said unto him: 'The counsel of Sammael against Manasseh is consummated: nought will avail thee.'

12. And on that day Hezekiah resolved in his heart to slay Manasseh his son.

13. And Isaiah said to Hezekiah: 'the Beloved hath made of none effect thy design, and the purpose of thy heart will not be accomplished, for with this calling have I been called and I shall inherit the heritage of the Beloved.'

### Chapter II

1. And it came to pass after that Hezekiah died and Manasseh became king, that he did not remember the commands of Hezekiah his father, but forgat them, and Sammael abode in Manasseh and clung fast to him.

2. And Manasseh forsook the service of the God of his father, and he served Satan and his angels and his powers.

3. And he turned aside the house of his father which had been before the face of Hezekiah from the words of wisdom and from the service of God.

4. And Manasseh turned aside his heart to serve Beliar; for this purpose Sammael swore by his name that he would turn aside all the righteousness of Hezekiah.

5. And there were two secretaries: Zadok, a doer of righteousness, and Tobia the son of Tobia, a teacher of the things that are ungodly.

6. And they wrote in the presence of Manasseh words of ungodliness, even all the unrighteousness of the peoples.

7. And the king commanded that Tobia should be set over all the people, and should write in his presence every day words of unrighteousness.

8. And Manasseh forsook the fear of the God of his father and did that which was evil in the sight of the Lord.

9. And all the people observed the commands of the king in all the kingdom of Manasseh.

10. And the people remembered not the judgement of Hezekiah.

11. And Manasseh did not depart from the counsel of Sammael, but he fulfilled all his desire.

12. And in those days the children of Israel observed the covenant of the Lord and the commands of the law of Moses.

13. And Manasseh caused Jerusalem to err, and the people, to act ungodlily like himself.

14. And the whole host of the firmament was grieved over the ungodliness of Manasseh.

15. And Sammael was glad over the ungodliness of Manasseh.

16. And there was sorrow in the whole creation over the ungodliness of Manasseh.

## Part II: The Testament of Hezekiah (Chapter III)

### Chapter III

1. And it came to pass in the fifteenth year of the reign of Hezekiah, in the month of Nisan, that the king was grieved, and he called all the chiefs and the priests and the prophets and the scribes to him.

2. And the king commanded that they should bring all the books that were in Jerusalem, for he wished to burn the strange gods.

3. And they brought all the books, and the king commanded that they should read the words of the books in the presence of the king.

4. And there was found in one of the books a writing concerning the descent of Sammael, and concerning the judgment of the world.

5. And the king heard the words, and his countenance was troubled.

6. And the king commanded that they should bring the book to him, and he read it.

7. And the king understood the things that were written therein.

8. And the king commanded that the book should be hidden.

9. And the king said to the priest: 'From where came the book?'

10. And the priest said: 'From the days of our fathers.'

11. And the king commanded that the book should be read in the presence of the people.

12. And the king commanded that all the people should be gathered together.

13. And all the people were gathered together, and the king read the book in their presence.

## Key Themes in the Ascension of Isaiah

### The Seven Heavens

The Ascension of Isaiah describes a detailed cosmology of seven concentric heavens:

1. **First Heaven** - The atmospheric heaven where clouds and winds are
2. **Second Heaven** - Where hostile powers and principalities dwell  
3. **Third Heaven** - Where the garden of Eden is located
4. **Fourth Heaven** - Where the sun, moon, and stars are positioned
5. **Fifth Heaven** - Where angels of fire and frost reside
6. **Sixth Heaven** - Where the angels of lightning and thunder are stationed
7. **Seventh Heaven** - The abode of God and the highest angels

Above and beyond all seven heavens is the **Great Glory** - the dwelling place of the Most High.

### The Descent of Satan

The text describes how **Sammael** (identified with Satan/Beliar) descended through the seven heavens:

- Sammael was originally one of the chief archangels
- He sought to make the earth his kingdom
- He tempted Eve in the Garden of Eden
- He became the chief of the Satans
- He is the angel of death
- He acts as the special foe of Israel
- He possesses Manasseh, King of Judah
- He orchestrates the persecution and martyrdom of Isaiah

### The Martyrdom of Isaiah

The text includes the account of Isaiah's martyrdom:

- Isaiah prophesies to King Hezekiah about Manasseh's wickedness
- Sammael enters into Manasseh
- Manasseh becomes a follower of Beliar
- Isaiah is sawed in half by a wooden saw
- This martyrdom is confirmed in other early Christian literature
