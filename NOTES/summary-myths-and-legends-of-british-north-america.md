# Myths and Legends of British North America: summary

## What the text is

This is a comparative collection of North American myths and legends. It is not a single indigenous source, but it preserves a broad range of motifs that are very useful for the base: creation, flood, spirit-world, and culture-hero patterns.

## What it does

The collection shows that North American traditions often organize myth around:

- the creation of the world or its reshaping
- flood narratives
- beings who cross between worlds
- local sacred places
- a spirit-world that overlaps with landscape

## Why it matters for the base

The collection is useful because it lets us compare American material with the Old World not as a derivative echo, but as its own strong mythic field. It is especially useful for:

- flood motifs
- hero / helper figures
- sacred geography
- world-formation through repeated or transformative acts
